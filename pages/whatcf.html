What is CF?

Cystic fibrosis was first identified as a specific disease in the year 1938, after it was distinguished from Celiac Disease. This was a result of the first medical report that was wrote about Cystic Fibrosis by Dorothy Andersen, who also was a huge assist in creating a test to diagnose cystic fibrosis.

Cystic fibrosis is a disease which is transmitted through genetic genes. In order for a CF patient to be born there needs to be two faulty copies of the genes, one from each parent to be transferred. According to Cystic Fibrosis Trust, there are more than 10,800 people affecting Cystic Fibrosis with in United Kingdom alone. Also, according to them one from each 25 people carries a faulty gene that is a cause of the disease and the gene exists without even knowing. CF affects the genes that controls the movement of salt and water in and out of the cells.

Symptoms of CF People with CF can have a variety of symptoms, including:

  • Very salty-tasting skin
  • Persistent coughing, at times with phlegm
  • Frequent lung infections including pneumonia or bronchitis
  • Wheezing or shortness of breath
  • Poor growth or weight gain in spite of a good appetite
  • Frequent greasy, bulky stools or difficulty with bowel movements
  • Male infertility
